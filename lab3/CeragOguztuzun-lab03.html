Question 3

From the plot in Question 1, It can be seen that the number of penguins that have a body mass between 2000 and 4000 is the highest, but apart from that the data is very symmetric. We can also observe this asymmetry by observing how median and mean are not very close to each other. The mean is higher than the median which prove the presence of outliers pulling the data down. The body mass follows a Normal distribution. It is right skewed as the mean is greater than the median. As the data is skewed, It is appropriate to examine the mean and interquartile range, we would examine median If the data were more symmetric.

Question 10

Regarding the analysis made in this lab using the dataset, adult obesity, and food insecurity are not strongly nor weekly correlated given the steepness of the loess curve of the scatter plot, the variance is very high in the scatter plot, which means food insecurity will not be a good candidate in predicting adult obesity.

Reflecting on Spiegelhalter, in order to state a causality, we have to establish a number of factors in our data. We should have control groups where other variables are held constant such that we can observe the relationship between food insecurity and adult obesity better. We should have an allocation of treatment, which means the comparisons groups in the data should be as similar as possible. If we narrow our analysis regarding these, the causality between adult obesity and food insecurity can be better analyzed.
